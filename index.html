About Me

I am currently a research assistant at the VinUni-Illinois Smart Health Center (VISHC), VinUniversity. My research focuses on developing deep learning algorithms to detect cardiovascular diseases using ECG signals.

I received my BSc degree in Electrical Engineering from Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST) in late 2020. Post graduation, I joined VinBigdata's AI Engineer training program and interned there for a year, conducting research on Medical Image Analysis. Since the beginning of 2022, I have done research at VinUniversity and explored for PhD opportunities.

I am looking for a Ph.D. position in Computer Science starting in Fall 2023, working on Few-shot Learning, Reinforcement Learning and Bayesian Optimization for Computer Vision problems.


Email / GitHub / LinkedIn / Google scholar / CV

Research interests

My research interests are few-shot learning, vision transformer, and Bayesian Optimization for Computer Vision problems (i.e. object detection, semantic/instance segmentation, object counting, OOD detection) and can be extended for continual learning task. In particular, my research goal is aiming to develop computer vision algorithms that can be generalized/optimized with minimal training data or human supervision and effectively shifted into other domains out-of-distribution.
